What are the sources of Brazilian coffee?

Coffee beans are grown on a tree called a coffee plant, which is a tropical plant that loves shade. Coffee plants flower only once during their life, and coffee beans are harvested from the seeds found Inside the coffee cherry that grows from the flower. Brazilian coffee cherries are red and delicious. Coffee Is harvested when the coffee cherry turns yellow or golden. There are different harvesting techniques that can be used during the harvesting process, the most common methods Include beating, picking and strip picking. Beating Involves a machine shaving the fruit off the tree, while picking means harvesting the coffee cherries by hand. Finally, strip picking Is a combination of beating and picking where the machine shaves the cherries first, then people pick the coffee beans from the shavings

Brazilian coffee is a type of dark roast coffee that originates from Brazil, and Is made from a mixture of Arabica and Robusta beans.
